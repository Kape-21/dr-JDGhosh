Real Patient Outcomes

Documented case studies showcasing successful recoveries from complex spinal and orthopaedic conditions.

Spinal Deformity

Scoliosis Correction

Patient 14-Year-Old Boy
Condition Idiopathic Scoliosis (60°)

Patient suffered from significant spinal curvature affecting posture and lung capacity. Traditional bracing had failed. Using a telescopic rod system, we corrected the curvature while allowing for future growth. The patient gained 3 inches in height immediately and returned to school in 3 weeks.

Trauma Recovery

Spinal Compression

Patient 45-Year-Old Male
Condition L1 Burst Fracture

After a severe fall, the patient lost sensation in his legs. MRI revealed a burst fracture compressing the spinal cord, threatening paralysis. Emergency decompression and stabilization were performed. Motor power returned by Day 5. He walked out of the hospital without support on Day 12.

Bilateral Knee Replacement

Patient 65-Year-Old Female
Condition Grade 4 Osteoarthritis

Confined to a wheelchair due to excruciating pain, she avoided surgery for years. Staged bilateral Total Knee Replacement was performed using muscle-sparing techniques. She mobilized the next day and, 6 months later, travels independently.
