Real Patient Outcomes

In-depth case studies showcasing complex challenges and successful recoveries.

Spinal Deformity

Scoliosis Correction

Patient: 14-Year-Old Boy
Condition: Idiopathic Scoliosis (60°)

Patient suffered from significant spinal curvature affecting posture and lung capacity. Traditional bracing had failed.

Outcome: Using a telescopic rod system, we corrected the curvature while allowing for future growth. The patient gained 3 inches in height immediately and returned to school in 3 weeks.

Trauma Recovery

Spinal Compression

Patient: 45-Year-Old Male
Condition: L1 Burst Fracture

After a severe fall, the patient lost sensation in his legs. MRI revealed a burst fracture compressing the spinal cord, threatening paralysis.

Outcome: Emergency decompression and stabilization performed. Motor power returned by Day 5. He walked out of the hospital without support on Day 12.

Joint Reconstruction

Bilateral Knee Replacement

Patient: 65-Year-Old Female
Condition: Grade 4 Osteoarthritis

Confined to a wheelchair due to excruciating pain, she avoided surgery for years. Her quality of life had deteriorated significantly.

Outcome: Staged bilateral Total Knee Replacement using muscle-sparing techniques. Mobilized the next day. 6 months later, she travels independently.
